1. Harry Potter and the Philospher's Stone

"Written by JK. Rowling"

Review: Harry Potter and the Philosopher's Stone is a fantastic journey into a magical world. Harry, a young boy living with his unkind relatives, discovers on his eleventh birthday that he is a wizard. He attends Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry, where he makes lifelong friends and encounters various magical creatures. The story is filled with excitement, mystery, and the joy of discovering a new world. It's an enchanting and captivating beginning to a beloved series that captures the imagination of readers.

rating - ★★★★★



2. The Fault in our Stars

"Written by John Green"

Review: The Fault in Our Stars tells the moving story of Hazel and Gus, two teenagers battling cancer who find love and companionship. Their relationship is heartwarming and tragic, filled with humor and deep conversations about life and mortality. It’s an emotional and beautifully written novel that captures the essence of love and loss.

rating - ★★★



3. It Ends with us

"Written by Colleen Hoover

Review: It Ends With Us is a compelling and emotional story about Lily, who falls in love with Ryle, a charming neurosurgeon. As their relationship deepens, Lily confronts painful memories and difficult decisions. The novel tackles themes of love, abuse, and resilience, making it a heartfelt and thought-provoking read.

rating - ★★★★



4. Atomic Habits

"Written by James Clear"

Review: Atomic Habits is an insightful book by James Clear that teaches how small changes can lead to big results. It offers practical advice on building good habits, breaking bad ones, and understanding the science behind behavior. The book is motivating and easy to follow, making it a great guide for personal improvement.

rating - ★★★



5. The Monk who Sold his Ferrari

"Written by Robin Sharma"

Review: The Monk Who Sold His Ferrari is an inspiring tale by Robin Sharma about a lawyer who leaves his fast-paced life to find peace and purpose. Through spiritual lessons and practical advice, it encourages readers to pursue a balanced, fulfilling life. It's motivating and thought-provoking, perfect for self-improvement.

rating - ★★★★★



6. Ugly Love

"Written by Colleen Hoover

Review: Ugly Love was first published in 2014, but it is currently sitting in Australia’s bestselling fiction charts thanks to its huge following on social media. My curiosity about the immense popularity of Ugly Love encouraged me to purchase this one, despite all the review books I have waiting for me! An emotional and gut punch style story from beginning to end, Ugly Love is a defining modern romance tale.

rating - ★★★



7. Ikigai

"Written by Héctor García and Francesc Miralles:

Review: No one likes getting old, but it is inevitable. The concept of death is a bit grim of course, but not when you bring in relativity. Imagine this — there’s a sign, far off in the future, with a number on it. And that number represents the age your death. Every year that you live, you advance closer to the sign — because the sign is stationary. So when you reach the sign, theoretically you die. But that’s boring.

rating - ★★★



8. Pride and Prejudice

"Written by Jane Austen"

Review: Elizabeth Bennet is a daring and free-spirited young woman misunderstood in the age of Regency-era England. The enigmatic and ravishingly rich bachelor Mr. Darcy develops a most profound fondness for her but is faced with an unprecedented reaction — a rejection from her side.

rating - ★★★



9. Seven Husbands of Evelyn Hugo

"Written by Taylor Jenkins Reid

Review: Seven Husbands Of Evelyn Hugo by Taylor Jenkins Reid More than that, though, this novel is so heavily tied to the concepts of identity and self appreciation that, though it is considered to be a romance and a historical fiction novel, I would classify it as a tale of self love and acceptance. Certainly, it's an excellent read for anyone who needs to feel something.

rating - ★★★



10. The Cruel Prince

"Written by Holly Black

Review: The Cruel Prince by Holly Black is a gripping fantasy about Jude, a mortal girl raised in the treacherous High Court of Faerie. Amidst political intrigue and deadly rivalries, Jude fights for power and survival. With vivid world-building and complex characters, it's a thrilling tale of ambition, betrayal, and resilience that keeps readers hooked until the final page.

rating - ★★★



11. The Night Circus

"Written by Erin Morgenstern"

Review: The Night Circus by Erin Morgenstern is a mesmerizing tale of a magical competition between two young illusionists, Celia and Marco. Set in a mystical circus that appears without warning, their rivalry evolves into a deep, forbidden love. With enchanting prose and vivid imagery, this atmospheric novel weaves a spellbinding story of love, destiny, and the power of imagination.

rating - ★★★★★



12. Film for her

"Written by Orion Carloto"

Review: Her is a thought-provoking film directed by Spike Jonze, depicting a futuristic love story between Theodore, a lonely writer, and Samantha, an intelligent operating system. Exploring themes of connection, loneliness, and identity, it challenges notions of human relationships and technology. Joaquin Phoenix's poignant portrayal and the film's poignant storytelling make it a poignant and introspective viewing experience.

rating - ★★★★★
